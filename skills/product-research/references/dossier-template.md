## 2. Copy Quotes

**File:** `{slug}-copy-quotes.md`
**Word count:** 600–1,000

### Structure

25+ verbatim customer quotes organized by theme. Each quote includes:

```markdown
## {Theme Name} (e.g., "Transformation / Results")

> "{Exact verbatim quote}"
> — Source: {Platform}, {context if available}
**Copy angle:** {How to use this quote in marketing}

> "{Another quote}"
> — Source: {Platform}
**Copy angle:** {Usage suggestion}
```

### Themes to Cover
- Transformation / Before-After
- Emotional response / Delight
- Specific features praised
- Gift-giving / Sharing moments
- Skeptic-turned-believer
- Comparison to alternatives
- Pain points (from negative reviews — useful for objection handling)

### Quality Criteria
- Quotes must be VERBATIM — no paraphrasing
- Include source platform (Amazon, Reddit, TikTok, YouTube, etc.)
- Prioritize vivid, emotional, specific language
- Include 3-5 negative quotes for objection handling

## 3. Market Landscape

```markdown

- **Headwinds:** What's slowing or threatening the category

## Key Players
| Brand | Est. Revenue | Positioning | Strength | Weakness |
|-------|-------------|-------------|----------|----------|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Category Trends
3-5 trends with evidence and implications for positioning.

## White Space Opportunities
Gaps in the market that the product can exploit.

## Regulatory / Compliance
Any relevant regulations, certifications, or compliance requirements.
```

## 4. Competitor Teardown

**File:** `{slug}-competitor-teardown.md`
**Word count:** 2,000–3,000

### Structure

Analyze 8-12 direct and adjacent competitors:

```markdown
# Competitor Teardown: {Product}

## Positioning Matrix
| Competitor | Price | Primary Claim | Unique Mechanism | Target Audience | Weakness |
|-----------|-------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Detailed Analysis

### {Competitor 1}
- **URL:** {link}
- **Price:** {price}
- **Positioning:** {their core promise}
- **Unique mechanism:** {what they claim makes them different}
- **Messaging style:** {tone, approach}
- **Strengths:** {what they do well}
- **Weaknesses:** {vulnerabilities to exploit}
- **Customer sentiment:** {what their reviewers say}
- **Ad strategy:** {if visible — Meta Ad Library, TikTok}

[Repeat for each competitor]

## Competitive Gaps
Opportunities based on competitor weaknesses and unmet customer needs.

## Positioning Recommendations
How to position against the competitive landscape.
```

### Research Requirements
- Check each competitor's website, Amazon listing, social media
- Pull actual pricing (not estimates)
- Check Meta Ad Library for active ads
- Read their reviews for VoC intelligence
- Note their unique mechanisms and claims

## 6. Objection Kill Sheet

**File:** `{slug}-objection-kill-sheet.md`
**Word count:** 1,800–2,800

### Structure

12-15 objections, each with full treatment:

```markdown
# Objection Kill Sheet: {Product}

## Objection Severity Matrix
| # | Objection | Frequency | Severity | Kill Difficulty |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | ... | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | Easy/Med/Hard |

## Detailed Kill Sheets

### Objection 1: "{Exact objection in customer's words}"

**Frequency:** {How often this appears}
**Source:** {Where you found it — reviews, Reddit, etc.}
**Root belief:** {The underlying belief driving this objection}

**Reframe:**
{The strategic reframe — how to shift their perspective}

**Proof points:**
1. {Specific evidence that counters the objection}
2. {Social proof, data, or demonstration}
3. {Authority or third-party validation}

**Copy angles:**
- {Ad hook that addresses this objection}
- {Email subject line}
- {Social proof format}

**Pre-emption strategy:**
{How to address this before it arises — in product page, ads, or content}

[Repeat for each objection]
```

### Objection Sources
- 1-3 star Amazon reviews
- Reddit complaint threads
- "Is {product} worth it?" queries
- Return reason data (if available)
- Customer service FAQ patterns
- Competitor comparison threads

## Formatting Standards (All Files)

- Use `##` for major sections, `###` for subsections
- Tables for comparative data
- Blockquotes (`>`) for verbatim customer quotes
- Bold for emphasis on key terms
- Bullet lists for scannable information
- Every claim linked to source (URL or platform reference)
- Wiki links (`[[filename]]`) for cross-references between dossier files
