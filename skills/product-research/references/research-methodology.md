## Phase 1: Market Landscape Research

### Objective
Understand the category context: size, growth, dynamics, trends, and key players.

### Process

1. **Category definition** — Define the product category broadly and narrowly
   - Broad: "home fitness equipment"
   - Narrow: "smart jump ropes"
   - Adjacent: "connected fitness accessories"

2. **Market sizing** — Search for market reports, industry analyses
   - Look for: TAM, growth rate, key segments
   - Sources: industry reports, news articles, investor presentations
   - If exact data unavailable, triangulate from multiple sources

3. **Trend identification** — Find 3-5 trends shaping the category
   - Consumer behavior shifts
   - Technology trends
   - Regulatory changes
   - Channel shifts (DTC growth, marketplace dynamics)
   - Each trend should have supporting evidence

4. **Category dynamics** — Map the competitive landscape
   - Who dominates? Who's growing?
   - Pricing spectrum (budget → premium)
   - Distribution channels
   - Seasonality patterns
   - Barrier to entry analysis

### Output
Market landscape file with category overview, dynamics, key players table, trends, and white space analysis.

## Phase 2: Competitive Intelligence

### Objective
Build a detailed picture of 8-12 competitors: their positioning, mechanisms, pricing, strengths, and weaknesses.

### Process

1. **Competitor identification**
   - Direct competitors (same product, same audience)
   - Indirect competitors (different product, same problem)
   - Aspirational competitors (where the market is heading)
   - Search: "best {category}", "{product} alternatives", "{product} vs"

2. **Per-competitor deep dive** (for each of 8-12):
   - Visit their website — note positioning, messaging, design quality
   - Check Amazon listing — pricing, reviews, star rating
   - Check Meta Ad Library — active ads, creative direction
   - Read 5-10 of their reviews — what do THEIR customers say?
   - Note pricing and offer structure

3. **Positioning matrix**
   - Map competitors on 2 axes relevant to the category
   - Common axes: price vs quality, features vs simplicity, niche vs broad
   - Identify positioning gaps (white space)

4. **Vulnerability analysis**
   - Per competitor: what do their negative reviews say?
   - Common weaknesses across the category
   - Positioning weaknesses (claims that aren't supported)
   - Service/experience gaps

### Output
Competitor teardown file with positioning matrix, per-competitor analysis, gaps, and positioning recommendations.

## Phase 3: Voice of Customer Mining

### Objective
Extract authentic customer language, emotions, purchase drivers, and objections from real reviews and discussions.

### Process

1. **Amazon review mining**
   - Find the product and top 3 competitors on Amazon
   - Read 20-30 reviews per product (mix of ratings)
   - Extract: verbatim quotes, emotional language, feature mentions, complaints
   - Pay special attention to 3-star reviews (most balanced/useful)
   - Check Q&A section for pre-purchase objections

2. **Reddit thread mining**
   - Search for product name and category in relevant subreddits
   - Read full threads (context matters more than individual comments)
   - Look for: comparison discussions, "is it worth it" threads, complaint threads
   - Reddit users tend to be more honest and detailed than Amazon reviewers

3. **TikTok/YouTube mining**
   - Search for reviews, unboxings, and "honest review" content
   - Focus on comment sections — often richer than the content itself
   - Note viral angles and hooks used by creators
   - Track which aspects generate the most engagement

4. **Quote extraction**
   - Record EXACT words (verbatim, including imperfect grammar)
   - Tag each quote with: source, sentiment (pos/neg/neutral), theme
   - Aim for 25+ quotes minimum, covering all major themes
   - Prioritize vivid, emotional, specific language over generic praise

5. **Theme synthesis**
   - Group quotes by theme: transformation, features, comparison, emotional
   - Identify frequency of each theme
   - Note language patterns (recurring words and phrases = ad copy gold)
   - Separate purchase motivations from satisfaction factors (not always the same)

### VoC Mining Strategies by Source

**Amazon — the "what"**
- Best for: feature feedback, specific complaints, comparison data
- Limitation: incentivized reviews, fake reviews
- Signal: verified purchase reviews, "most helpful" sorting

**Reddit — the "why"**
- Best for: authentic opinions, decision criteria, comparative analysis
- Limitation: skews younger/more tech-savvy
- Signal: highly upvoted comments, detailed personal experiences

**TikTok — the "vibe"**
- Best for: emotional reactions, viral potential, demographic signals
- Limitation: performative, trend-driven
- Signal: genuine comments, not creator responses

**YouTube — the "deep dive"**
- Best for: detailed product evaluation, long-term reviews, demonstrations
- Limitation: sponsored content
- Signal: comments from actual buyers, follow-up reviews

### Output
VoC analysis file + copy quotes file with 25+ verbatim quotes organized by theme.

## Phase 4: Persona Development

### Objective
Create 3-5 data-driven personas grounded in VoC research, not assumptions.

### Process

1. **Pattern identification**
   - Review all VoC data for natural audience segments
   - Look for distinct purchase motivations, not just demographics
   - Common segmentation axes:
     - Self-purchase vs gift-giver
     - First-time buyer vs upgrader
     - Price-sensitive vs quality-focused
     - Problem-driven vs aspiration-driven

2. **Persona construction** (per persona)
   - **Ground in data:** Every persona attribute should link to VoC evidence
   - **Demographics:** Age, gender, income (derived from review context clues)
   - **Psychographics:** Values, lifestyle, identity (from language patterns)
   - **Purchase trigger:** What event prompts them to start looking
   - **Decision criteria:** Ranked priorities in their purchase decision
   - **Awareness level:** Where they sit on the Schwartz awareness spectrum
   - **Belief chain:** Map the full belief chain from current state to purchase
   - **Channel affinity:** Where to reach them

3. **Persona validation**
   - Each persona should have at least 3-5 supporting quotes from VoC data
   - Personas should be distinct (if two are too similar, merge them)
   - At least one persona should be a non-obvious segment (gift-giver, parent buying for child, etc.)

4. **Persona prioritization**
   - Primary: largest segment or highest-value customers
   - Secondary: meaningful segments worth targeting
   - Each persona gets a custom messaging approach

### Output
Persona positioning file with overview table, detailed personas, belief chains, and per-persona messaging.

## Phase 5: Messaging Development

### Objective
Synthesize research into actionable messaging: hierarchy, ad angles, hooks, and content strategies.

### Process

1. **Core message extraction**
   - From all research, identify the single most compelling message
   - Test: would this message work in a 6-word headline?
   - Support with 3-4 secondary messages

2. **Unique mechanism identification**
   - Review product features, brand story, manufacturing process
   - Identify what's genuinely different (not just claimed)
   - Craft UMP (one-sentence proposition) and UMS (narrative story)
   - If no clear mechanism exists, help identify one

3. **Ad angle development** (10-15 angles)
   - Per angle, define: hook, angle, target persona, awareness level, format, platform
   - Ensure coverage across:
     - All awareness levels (at least 2 angles per level)
     - All personas (at least 2 angles per persona)
     - Multiple formats (UGC, polished, comparison, story, demonstration)
   - Each angle should be specific enough to brief a media buyer or content creator

4. **Content hook development**
   - 5-10 organic content hooks for social media
   - Based on VoC language and viral TikTok patterns
   - Format: "Hook text" + brief concept description

5. **Gift/occasion positioning** (if applicable)
   - How to position for gifting scenarios
   - Seasonal angles and timing
   - Gift-giver messaging (different from self-purchaser)

### Output
Messaging & ad angles file with hierarchy, unique mechanism, 10-15 angles, content hooks, and occasion positioning.

## Phase 6: Pricing & Offer Strategy

### Objective
Analyze pricing landscape and develop offer recommendations.

### Process

1. **Competitive price mapping**
   - Record actual prices for all competitors analyzed
   - Calculate price-per-unit where applicable
   - Note any subscription/membership pricing

2. **Perceived value analysis**
   - From VoC data: what do customers say about value?
   - What justifies the price? What undermines it?
   - Price sensitivity signals (mentions of "expensive", "worth it", "deal")

3. **Tier architecture design**
   - Entry tier: what's the minimum viable offer?
   - Core tier: what's the standard offering?
   - Premium tier: what maximizes AOV?
   - Each tier needs clear value differentiation

4. **Bundle strategy**
   - Natural product pairings (from "customers also bought" data)
   - Bundle savings framing
   - Cross-sell and upsell paths

5. **Promotional strategy**
   - Key promotional periods for the category
   - Launch pricing recommendations
   - Subscription potential (for consumables)

### Output
Pricing & bundling file with competitive table, value analysis, tier architecture, bundles, and promotional strategy.

## Quality Rubric

### Research Quality Checks

| Dimension | Minimum | Standard | Excellent |
|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Sources consulted | 15+ | 30+ | 50+ |
| Verbatim quotes | 15+ | 25+ | 40+ |
| Competitors analyzed | 5+ | 8-12 | 12-15 |
| Objections identified | 8+ | 12-15 | 15-20 |
| Personas developed | 2-3 | 3-5 | 5-7 |
| Ad angles created | 5-8 | 10-15 | 15-20 |
| All claims sourced | Mostly | Yes | Yes + linked |

### Common Quality Failures

1. **Generic insights** — "Customers value quality" tells us nothing. Be specific: "Customers repeatedly mention the weight of the product as a quality signal (14 mentions in 30 reviews)."

2. **Assumed personas** — "Working moms aged 30-40" without VoC evidence. Ground every persona attribute in actual data.

3. **Recycled competitor info** — Copying the competitor's own claims without verification. Check reviews to see if claims match reality.

4. **Missing negative data** — Only extracting positive quotes. Negative reviews are often MORE valuable for messaging.

5. **Vague ad angles** — "Social proof ad" isn't an angle. "3-star review compilation showing skeptics who were won over after 2 weeks" is an angle.

6. **Unsourced claims** — Every assertion about the market, competitor, or customer should trace back to a specific source.
